## LEARN-PM-008
**Timestamp:** Thursday, 17-Apr-2026 · IST
**Keywords:** prd, assumptions, unconfirmed
**Domain:** product_management
**Topic:** prd_writing
**Source:** General

**Context:**
Documenting the most common PRD failure mode from capability 11.

**Learning:**
Never draft any PRD section before the user has explicitly confirmed
the brainstormed requirements. Writing PRD content on assumptions —
even reasonable ones — causes full rewrites after user review.

**Why It Matters:**
A PRD written on unconfirmed assumptions becomes incorrect the moment
the user corrects a single assumption. The correction cascades through
the entire document. Full rewrites cost 3–5x the initial writing time.

**Apply When:**
Any time there is pressure to "just start writing the PRD." Pause.
Ask: "Has the user confirmed the feature list from brainstorming?"
If no — brainstorm first. If yes — proceed.

## LEARN-PM-009
**Timestamp:** Thursday, 17-Apr-2026 · IST
**Keywords:** prd, open-questions, skipping
**Domain:** product_management
**Topic:** prd_writing
**Source:** General

**Context:**
Documenting the open questions requirement from capability 11.

**Learning:**
Never skip the Open Questions section of a PRD. Unanswered questions
left out of the PRD surface as blockers during development — at the
worst possible moment.

**Why It Matters:**
An open question discovered during build costs 10x more to resolve
than one identified during PRD. Development must stop, the question
must be answered, and work may need to be undone.

**Apply When:**
Every PRD creation. After the tech stack section, always add an
Open Questions section. Even "no open questions" should be stated
explicitly — it signals thorough thinking.
